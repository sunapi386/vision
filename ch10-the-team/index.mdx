# Chapter 10: The Team and the Journey

Every startup has a origin story. Most of them are myths — polished narratives designed to impress investors and journalists. This chapter is the real version: how a three-person bootstrapped team built a platform that beat IBM on a government procurement, earned the trust of police services and universities, and did it all without a single dollar of external funding.

The story starts with a founder who spent a decade at Apple, Amazon, and an early-stage self-driving car startup — then walked away from the career ladder to build something from scratch. It continues through the financial discipline of bootstrapping, the evidence we've accumulated, and the culture that makes a small team punch far above its weight.

## Sections

- [The Founder Story](./01-founder-story.mdx) — UWaterloo to Apple to Amazon to AutoX to founder. A decade of shipping production AI systems, and the identity shift from engineer to builder of businesses and ecosystems.

- [Bootstrapping Philosophy](./02-bootstrapping-philosophy.mdx) — No external funding. $25K/month burn. Consulting revenue funds product development. Why building with constraints produces better products than building with someone else's money.

- [What We Proved](./03-what-we-proved.mdx) — Alberta RFP win over IBM and Deloitte. Three police services piloting. SJSU partnership. McMaster advisory committee. $20M GPU deployment. The receipts.

- [Culture](./04-culture.mdx) — Open source by default. Protocol-first thinking. Nix reproducible builds. Three people doing the work of thirty. How and why it works.
