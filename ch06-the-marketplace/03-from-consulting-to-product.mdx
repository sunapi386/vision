# From Consulting to Product

## One-Offs and Annuities

Two retired BDO partners at the McMaster Forge framed it in terms that stuck: "one-offs and annuities."

Custom work for revenue. Repeatable work for cashflow. Every business needs both, but the ratio determines whether you are building an asset or running on a treadmill.

A consulting firm that does 100% custom work has revenue but no leverage. Every dollar of revenue requires a proportional dollar of labor. When the partners stop working, the revenue stops. When a key person leaves, their clients go with them. The firm is a collection of individual practices wearing a shared letterhead.

A consulting firm that converts its best engagements into repeatable workflows has both revenue and leverage. The custom work generates the insights. The repeatable workflows generate the cashflow. The custom work requires senior expertise. The repeatable workflows require oversight but not full-time involvement. The firm builds compounding value -- each engagement makes the workflow portfolio better.

This is the fundamental business model shift that the marketplace enables. Not from consulting to SaaS. From consulting to consulting-plus-product. The consulting extends runway and generates insights. The product captures those insights into recurring revenue.

## Every Engagement Should Produce Two Things

When a consultant finishes an engagement, they should have two deliverables: the work product for the client, and the workflow for themselves.

The work product is what the client paid for. A completed tax return. A drafted contract. A processed dataset. An implemented integration. This is the one-off. It has a defined scope, a deadline, and a fee.

The workflow is the reusable artifact. The process that produced the work product, encoded in the workflow engine, stripped of client-specific data, and generalized for broader application. This is the seed of the annuity.

Not every engagement produces a useful workflow. Some engagements are genuinely one-off -- a unique situation that will never repeat. But most consulting work falls into patterns. The eightieth tax return is not eighty times different from the first. The thirtieth contract review covers the same clauses with different details. The patterns are the workflows.

The discipline is to recognize the pattern during the engagement and encode it before moving on. This requires a platform that makes encoding easy -- visual, immediate, and testable. If encoding a workflow requires a week of software development after the engagement, it will not happen. The consultant is already on to the next project. The pattern is lost.

The AceTeam Workflow Engine is designed for this exact use case. During an engagement, the consultant builds the process as a workflow on the platform. They use it to do the actual work -- the workflow is not a separate documentation exercise, it is the tool they use. When the engagement ends, the workflow already exists. It was built as a side effect of doing the work.

## The Conversion Funnel

Here is how a consulting engagement converts into a marketplace product:

**Stage 1: Custom engagement.** A client hires you to process their SR&ED claim. You do the work on AceTeam, building the process as a workflow. The client pays for the engagement. You deliver the filing.

**Stage 2: Refinement.** A second client needs the same service. You use the workflow from the first engagement, adapting it for the new client's specifics. The workflow handles 70% of the work. You handle 30% manually. After the engagement, you incorporate the new edge cases into the workflow.

**Stage 3: Maturation.** By the fifth client, the workflow handles 90% of the work. The manual effort is down to expert review at human-in-the-loop gates. The time per engagement has dropped from forty hours to five. Your effective hourly rate has quadrupled.

**Stage 4: Marketplace.** The workflow is mature enough to publish. You strip out client-specific configurations and replace them with parameters. You write a description, set a price, and list it on the marketplace. New clients can purchase the workflow directly, without engaging you for custom work. You receive revenue on every execution.

**Stage 5: Scale.** The workflow runs hundreds of times per month across dozens of clients. You spend a few hours per week reviewing flagged cases and improving the workflow. The rest of your time is spent on new engagements that will produce new workflows.

This is the flywheel. Consulting generates expertise. Expertise generates workflows. Workflows generate passive revenue. Passive revenue funds more consulting. Each cycle compounds.

## Consulting Extends Runway

For a bootstrapped company, consulting is not a distraction from building the product. It is the bridge.

When we say AceTeam was built with zero external funding, the natural question is: how did you pay for it? The answer is consulting. Custom AI engagements for clients who needed workflows built, agents configured, or AI strategies developed. Each engagement paid the bills for a few more months of platform development.

But consulting alone is a treadmill. You bill by the hour, deliver the work, and start over. The hours you spend on consulting are hours you do not spend on the product. The tension is real and unsolvable -- unless every consulting engagement produces a reusable artifact.

That is the model we built. Every client engagement is an opportunity to build and refine a workflow. The engagement pays the bills. The workflow extends the product. The product enables more consulting at higher rates, because you are not starting from scratch -- you are configuring an existing system.

The economics shift over time. In year one, 90% of revenue is consulting and 10% is product. In year two, 70% consulting and 30% product. In year three, the ratio inverts. The consulting becomes the marketing funnel for the product. You do a custom engagement to demonstrate capability, produce a workflow as a byproduct, and convert the client (and future clients with similar needs) to marketplace subscriptions.

## The Dom and Rino Insight

The retired partners at the Forge articulated something that most founders miss: the distinction between revenue and cashflow.

Revenue is what comes in from work performed. Cashflow is what comes in reliably, predictably, without proportional labor. A law firm with forty active matters has revenue. A law firm with forty active matters and ten marketplace workflows generating $5,000 per month each has revenue and cashflow.

The cashflow is what gives you options. It funds the experiments -- trying a new market, hiring a specialist, investing in a feature. It absorbs the shocks -- a client delays payment, a deal falls through, a quarter is slow. Without cashflow, every disruption is a crisis. With cashflow, disruptions are inconveniences.

The marketplace turns one-offs into annuities. Every consulting engagement that produces a reusable workflow is a conversion from one-off revenue to annuity cashflow. The engagement pays once. The workflow pays monthly. The accumulation of workflows builds a portfolio of recurring revenue that stabilizes the business.

This is not unique to AceTeam. It is the natural business model for any domain expert who can encode their expertise into a reusable system. But AceTeam provides the platform that makes the encoding practical and the distribution automatic.

## The Compounding Effect

Consider a consultancy that produces two marketplace workflows per quarter. After one year, they have eight workflows. After two years, sixteen. After three years, twenty-four.

If each workflow generates an average of $3,000 per month in marketplace revenue (after the platform fee), the math is:

- End of year one: 8 workflows x $3,000/month = $24,000/month recurring
- End of year two: 16 workflows x $3,000/month = $48,000/month recurring
- End of year three: 24 workflows x $3,000/month = $72,000/month recurring

These numbers are illustrative, not guaranteed. The actual revenue per workflow depends on the domain, the market size, the pricing, and the quality. Some workflows will generate $500 per month. Some will generate $50,000 per month. The point is the compounding structure: each new workflow adds incremental recurring revenue on top of the existing base.

Meanwhile, the consulting engagements continue at their own pace, generating immediate revenue and producing the next workflow. The two streams -- consulting and marketplace -- reinforce each other. The consulting generates the expertise and the credentials that make marketplace workflows credible. The marketplace generates the recurring revenue that allows the consultancy to be selective about which engagements they take.

After three years, the firm has options. They can continue consulting and growing the portfolio. They can hire junior staff to handle marketplace reviews while seniors focus on new engagements. They can sell the workflow portfolio as an asset. They can license the entire portfolio to a larger firm.

These options exist because workflows are assets. Not hours billed. Not reports delivered. Assets that generate value independently of the creator's ongoing labor.
