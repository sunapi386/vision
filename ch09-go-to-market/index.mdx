# Chapter 9: Go-to-Market

Three channels — education, consulting, and product — and how they feed each other.

Most AI companies pick one go-to-market channel. They are either a product company (self-serve signups, PLG motions, growth teams) or a services company (founder-led sales, scoped engagements, referral networks). They are rarely both, because the conventional wisdom says services do not scale and product does not close.

We run all three channels because they compound. Education generates awareness and credibility. Awareness generates consulting inbound. Consulting generates revenue, case studies, and product insights. Case studies generate more consulting. Product insights produce reusable workflows. Workflows populate the marketplace. The marketplace generates passive revenue. Revenue funds more education.

Each channel has a distinct function, a distinct customer profile, and a distinct economics. Education is a loss leader that builds the top of the funnel. Consulting is a profit center that validates the product and generates the stories that sell it. The product is the scalable layer that turns everything else into a platform business.

This chapter traces each channel and then shows how they connect into a flywheel that, at sufficient velocity, becomes self-sustaining.
