# Chapter 11: The Future

This is the chapter where we look up from the keyboard and ask: where does all of this go?

The honest answer is that nobody knows. Anyone who claims certainty about the five-to-ten year trajectory of AI is selling something. But uncertainty doesn't mean we can't reason about direction. The trends are legible even if the destination isn't precise. Hardware gets cheaper. Models get better. Regulation gets stricter. Protocols emerge and ecosystems form around them. These forces have momentum.

What follows is our best thinking on the long arc — the singularity question, the form factor that AI compute takes, the transformation of the workforce, the case for distributed infrastructure, and the specific bets we're making with this company. It ends with the most honest accounting I can give of what we're risking and what we expect to gain.
