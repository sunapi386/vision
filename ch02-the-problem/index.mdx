# Chapter 2: The Problem

Three structural barriers prevent organizations from truly owning their AI capability. These aren't product gaps that a better chatbot can fill. They're architectural failures in how the AI industry is built.

The first is the cloud AI trap: per-token rent on someone else's intelligence, with pricing that ratchets, terms that shift, data exposure you can't control, and switching costs that deepen with every integration. The second is the workflow gap: organizations don't need chatbots — they need systems that ingest documents, apply rules, route approvals, and produce auditable output. Nothing on the market delivers this. The third is accountability: when AI agents cross organizational boundaries, there's no cost breakdown, no data provenance, no governance framework. The agent economy is running without paperwork.

Underneath all three problems, one trend is quietly reshaping the economics: GPU hardware is crossing over. Running your own inference, once impractical, is now cheaper than renting. The hardware is ready. The models are ready. What's missing is the software to make sovereign AI usable.
