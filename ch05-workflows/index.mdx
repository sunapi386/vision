# Chapter 5: Workflows as the Unit of Work

The previous chapter described the machinery. This chapter describes the insight that makes the machinery valuable.

The fundamental shift in AI is not that machines can answer questions. It is that machines can follow processes. A chatbot answers one question at a time. A workflow executes a forty-step process with checkpoints, branching logic, human review gates, and data transformations -- consistently, every time, at machine speed.

This changes the unit of automation. We are not automating tasks. We are automating workflows. And that distinction determines everything: what gets built, who builds it, how it gets sold, and what it is worth.

A task is: "summarize this document." A workflow is: "receive the document, classify its type, extract key fields, validate against a schema, check for duplicates, route anomalies to human review, generate a summary, format it for the client, send it by email, and log the result." The task is a feature. The workflow is a business.

This chapter argues that workflows are the natural unit of work in the AI era -- that they encode domain expertise into reproducible pipelines, that they follow the same hypothesis-testing discipline as science, that human oversight is a design requirement rather than a fallback, and that the best test of a product is whether you use it yourself.
